## **PART ONE: INTRO TO DEALFLOW AI**

**Q: WHAT IS IT?**

**Dealflow Ai** is going to be both a web and mobile based application \- after we nail the web application we will start working on the mobile version. There are several moving parts that make up this **“Off Market Deal Finder”**, but essentially works on behalf of business brokers and other individuals that occupy an industry along that same vertical. We will be employing an ensemble machine learning module set with a very specific 55+ weighted scoring metric that it will apply against data points ingested from around a dozen different sources via API. All data ingested will form entries in our data base where a 3 stage feedback loop will feed the action/outcome of the scored leads back into the ensemble to keep the scoring metric sharp and continue to pump out accurate off market leads. This is a subscription based service with 2 Subscription Tiers to choose from. Each Tier pays a set up fee and a monthly retainer. When we receive organic leads from either visits to our front end website or via our social media platform channels, the user signs up, pays and goes through onboarding to obtain access to their custom dashboard and they will then have certain capabilities dependent upon the Tier Subscription they are signed up for. The goal of the system is to provide a better and more accurate way to source off market leads for businesses showing signs or markers of an entity that might end up for sale by owner. The business brokers today are finding they are struggling with having an overage of buyers and not enough sellers- unfortunately owners make the mistake of letting their business go when falling on hard times rather than selling it into the marketplace. The same goes for when a business owner passes away and leaves their entity to a beneficiary who is not willing to take it on and lets it go rather than attempting to run it through the marketplace. So we are attempting to catch these businesses before they fall at the first sign of failure and match these brokers with owners and get a contract made. 

**Q: WHO IS IT FOR:** 

As I have stated, the platform is geared towards Business Brokers looking for those off market deals by using weighted metrics to score leads and matching them with potential sellers before the they hit the market or the owner even knows they are ready to sell. More often than not the owner starts searching for solutions casually when they are wanting to exit or are planning for an exit in the near future. If we can lock down an accuracy rate of 85% with our Business Brokers we can then expand our reach into the adjacent industries such as commercial and residential real estate, insurance professionals, estate and trust firms including probate attorney’s, accountants and private family offices. We may open up to investors later on down the road but for the foreseeable future we are just hoping to make capturing potential leads for our business broker clients immeasurably easier and at the same time mitigating the tremendous loss of revenue producing small and medium sized entities within our communities and even spanning outward to reach the multitude of enterprise level afflicted organizations here in the United States.

### **PART 2: DATABASE SCHEMA FOR RAW DATA INGESTION**

This is the foundation. Every business gets a profile. Every signal gets tracked with timestamps.

\-- \=====================================================  
\-- CORE BUSINESS ENTITY (The Master Record)  
\-- \=====================================================  
CREATE TABLE businesses (  
    id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen\_random\_uuid(),  
      
    \-- Identity  
    pdl\_company\_id VARCHAR(100) UNIQUE,          \-- People Data Labs ID (primary key for matching)  
    duns\_number VARCHAR(9),                       \-- D\&B identifier  
    ein VARCHAR(10),                              \-- Federal EIN if available  
      
    \-- Basic Info  
    legal\_name VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,  
    dba\_name VARCHAR(255),  
    business\_type VARCHAR(50),                    \-- LLC, Corp, Partnership, Sole Prop  
    naics\_code VARCHAR(6),  
    sic\_code VARCHAR(4),  
    industry\_category VARCHAR(100),  
      
    \-- Location  
    street\_address VARCHAR(255),  
    city VARCHAR(100),  
    state VARCHAR(2),  
    zip\_code VARCHAR(10),  
    county VARCHAR(100),  
    latitude DECIMAL(10, 7),  
    longitude DECIMAL(10, 7),  
      
    \-- Business Metrics (from PDL \+ D\&B)  
    founded\_date DATE,  
    years\_in\_business INTEGER GENERATED ALWAYS AS (  
        EXTRACT(YEAR FROM CURRENT\_DATE) \- EXTRACT(YEAR FROM founded\_date)  
    ) STORED,  
    employee\_count INTEGER,  
    employee\_count\_range VARCHAR(20),             \-- '1-10', '11-50', '51-200', etc.  
    revenue\_estimate DECIMAL(15, 2),  
    revenue\_range VARCHAR(30),                    \-- '\<$1M', '$1-5M', '$5-10M', etc.  
      
    \-- Contact  
    main\_phone VARCHAR(20),  
    website VARCHAR(255),  
      
    \-- Status  
    entity\_status VARCHAR(20) DEFAULT 'active',   \-- active, dissolved, suspended  
    data\_quality\_score DECIMAL(3, 2),             \-- 0.00-1.00 confidence in data  
      
    \-- Timestamps  
    created\_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),  
    updated\_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),  
    last\_enriched\_at TIMESTAMPTZ,  
      
    \-- Indexes for fast lookups  
    CONSTRAINT idx\_state\_industry UNIQUE (state, naics\_code, id)  
);

CREATE INDEX idx\_businesses\_location ON businesses(state, city);  
CREATE INDEX idx\_businesses\_industry ON businesses(naics\_code);  
CREATE INDEX idx\_businesses\_revenue ON businesses(revenue\_estimate);  
CREATE INDEX idx\_businesses\_age ON businesses(years\_in\_business);

\-- \=====================================================  
\-- BUSINESS OWNER (One-to-Many: Business can have multiple owners)  
\-- \=====================================================  
CREATE TABLE business\_owners (  
    id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen\_random\_uuid(),  
    business\_id UUID REFERENCES businesses(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,  
      
    \-- Identity  
    pdl\_person\_id VARCHAR(100),                   \-- People Data Labs Person ID  
      
    \-- Basic Info  
    first\_name VARCHAR(100),  
    last\_name VARCHAR(100),  
    full\_name VARCHAR(255),  
      
    \-- Demographics (Critical for scoring)  
    date\_of\_birth DATE,  
    age INTEGER GENERATED ALWAYS AS (  
        EXTRACT(YEAR FROM AGE(CURRENT\_DATE, date\_of\_birth))  
    ) STORED,  
    gender VARCHAR(20),  
      
    \-- Contact  
    personal\_email VARCHAR(255),  
    business\_email VARCHAR(255),  
    mobile\_phone VARCHAR(20),  
    linkedin\_url VARCHAR(500),  
      
    \-- Address  
    home\_street VARCHAR(255),  
    home\_city VARCHAR(100),  
    home\_state VARCHAR(2),  
    home\_zip VARCHAR(10),  
      
    \-- Ownership  
    ownership\_percentage DECIMAL(5, 2),  
    role VARCHAR(100),                            \-- Owner, Partner, CEO, President  
    is\_primary\_owner BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE,  
    ownership\_start\_date DATE,  
      
    \-- Timestamps  
    created\_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),  
    updated\_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),  
      
    UNIQUE(business\_id, pdl\_person\_id)  
);

CREATE INDEX idx\_owners\_age ON business\_owners(age);  
CREATE INDEX idx\_owners\_business ON business\_owners(business\_id);

    event\_date DATE,  
    event\_source VARCHAR(100),                    \-- 'unicourt', 'obituary\_scrape', 'court\_record'  
      
    \-- Event Details  
    case\_number VARCHAR(100),  
    court\_name VARCHAR(255),  
    jurisdiction VARCHAR(100),  
      
    \-- Status  
    event\_status VARCHAR(50),                     \-- 'filed', 'pending', 'finalized'

#### **ENSEMBLE SCORING LOGIC**

def calculate\_ensemble\_score(business\_id: str) \-\> dict:  
    """  
    Calculate final score using weighted ensemble.  
    Returns score 0-100 and tier assignment.  
    """  
      
    \# Extract all features for this business  
    features \= extract\_features(business\_id)  \# Returns dict of 55 features  
    feature\_vector \= np.array(\[features\[key\] for key in FEATURE\_KEYS\])  
      
    \# Scale for neural network  
    feature\_vector\_scaled \= scaler.transform(feature\_vector.reshape(1, \-1))  
      
    \# Get predictions from each model  
    xgb\_pred \= xgboost\_model.predict(feature\_vector.reshape(1, \-1))\[0\]  
    rf\_pred \= random\_forest\_model.predict(feature\_vector.reshape(1, \-1))\[0\]  
    nn\_pred \= neural\_net\_model.predict(feature\_vector\_scaled)\[0\]  
      
    \# Weighted ensemble  
    ensemble\_weights \= {  
        'xgboost': 0.42,  
        'random\_forest': 0.33,  
        'neural\_net': 0.25  
    }  
      
    final\_score \= (  
        xgb\_pred \* ensemble\_weights\['xgboost'\] \+  
        rf\_pred \* ensemble\_weights\['random\_forest'\] \+  
        nn\_pred \* ensemble\_weights\['neural\_net'\]  
    )  
      
    \# Clip to 0-100 range  
    final\_score \= np.clip(final\_score, 0, 100\)  
      
    \# Assign tier based on score  
    if final\_score \>= 75:  
        tier \= 'platinum'       \# 0-3 months \- IMMEDIATE  
        urgency \= 'immediate'  
    elif final\_score \>= 55:  
        tier \= 'gold'           \# 3-6 months \- HIGH PRIORITY  
        urgency \= 'high'  
    elif final\_score \>= 35:  
        tier \= 'silver'         \# 6-12 months \- NURTURE  
        urgency \= 'medium'  
    else:  
        tier \= 'unqualified'    \# Not worth pursuing now  
        urgency \= 'low'  
      
    return {  
        'business\_id': business\_id,  
        'final\_score': round(final\_score, 2),  
        'tier': tier,  
        'urgency': urgency,  
        'model\_predictions': {  
            'xgboost': round(xgb\_pred, 2),  
            'random\_forest': round(rf\_pred, 2),  
            'neural\_net': round(nn\_pred, 2\)  
        },  
        'top\_contributing\_signals': get\_top\_signals(features),  \# SHAP explainability  
        'scored\_at': datetime.utcnow().isoformat()  
    }

### **PART 7: 3-STAGE FEEDBACK LOOP EXPLANATION**

**The 3-Stage Feedback Loop captures detailed outcomes at three critical stages and teaches the model: outreach attempts, calendar booking, deal closing. All data flows into the training system to continuously improve model accuracy.**

**Stage 1: Lead Outreach → Booked or Not Booked**  
**Outcome Options:**  
**Booked \-** Appointment successfully scheduled  
Optional notes field  
Proceeds to Stage 2  
Training data: outcome\_quality\_score \= 0.8  
**Not Booked \- No Answer \- Three call attempts completed with no contact**  
Detailed tracking: Attempt 1 (Tuesday 10 AM), Attempt 2 (Friday 10 AM), Attempt 3 (Monday 2 PM)  
Lead marked inactive  
Automatic retry scheduled in ninety days  
Stays with assigned broker  
Training data: outcome\_quality\_score \= 0.1  
**Not Booked \- Not Interested \- Explicit rejection from owner**  
Required open text field for specific reason (e.g., "Already working with another broker", "Business doing too well to sell", "Not planning to sell within five years")  
Lead marked inactive  
Training data: outcome\_quality\_score \= 0.2  
**Not Booked \- DNC (Do Not Contact) \- Owner requests no further contact**  
Lead permanently removed from outreach  
Retained in database for compliance records  
Training data: outcome\_quality\_score \= 0.0  
**Not Booked \- Future Interest \- Owner provides callback timeframe**  
AI agent or broker captures specific callback date from owner  
Lead remains active/pending on broker dashboard with callback date flagged  
NOT marked inactive  
System automatically schedules follow-up at specified time  
Broker or AI reaches out again at callback date  
Training data: outcome\_quality\_score \= 0.6

**Stage 2: Booked → Closed or Not Closed**  
After broker meets with prospect:  
**Closed \- Won \- Contract successfully signed**  
Signature obtained on Broker Contract  
Commission amount entered (only after actual closing, not at meeting)  
Full details logged for reporting  
Training data: outcome\_quality\_score \= 1.0  
**Closed \- Lost \- Deal did not proceed**  
Required open text field for reason (e.g., "Valuation expectations misaligned", "Decided not to sell after all", "Chose different broker", "Timing not right for sale")  
Training data: outcome\_quality\_score \= 0.3  
**Future \- Pending \- Prospect needs more time to decide**  
Broker enters note with expected timeline (e.g., "Will be ready in thirty days")  
Lead stays active on broker dashboard  
Broker continues receiving new weekly lead batches  
Broker responsible for following up at appropriate time  
No training data added until final outcome

**Stage 3: Contract Signed → Deal Closed**  
On/After Closing Day  
**Complete/Deal Closed/Funded \- Archive Lead**  
Transaction completed, closing docs signed and deal has been funded  
Commission Amount Entered  
Any additional details Broker wants to log for reporting or historical purposes  
After close out “Lead” tag is changed to “Previous” and dropped into the Client database in the CRM/Dashboard under respective tab for later follow up or use

def prepare\_training\_data\_from\_feedback():  
    """  
    Convert closed feedback loops into training examples.  
    """  
      
    query \= """  
    SELECT   
        sl.id,  
        sl.feature\_vector,  
        sl.original\_score,  
        sl.tier,

        df.actual\_days\_to\_sale,

        of.outcome as outreach\_outcome  
    FROM scored\_leads sl  
    JOIN deal\_feedback df ON sl.id \= df.lead\_id  
    LEFT JOIN meeting\_feedback mf ON sl.id \= mf.lead\_id  
    LEFT JOIN outreach\_feedback of ON sl.id \= of.lead\_id  
    WHERE df.outcome IN ('sold', 'listing\_expired', 'withdrawn')  
    AND df.created\_at \> NOW() \- INTERVAL '12 months'  
    """

            if row.actual\_days\_to\_sale \<= 90:

            elif row.actual\_days\_to\_sale \<= 180:
